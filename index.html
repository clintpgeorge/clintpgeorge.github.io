Clint P. George

About Me
I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer and Information Science and Engineering, University of Florida . My doctoral advisers are Dr. Joseph N. Wilson and Dr. Hani Doss.

My research interests include:

  • Empirical Bayes methods
  • Markov chain Monte Carlo methods
  • Large scale machine learning


I am also collaborating with Dr. Daisy Zhe Wang in the domain of large scale machine learning at the Data Science Research lab and Database Systems Research Center, University of Florida.

Before joining the doctoral program, I was a master's student at the Department of Computer and Information Science and Engineering, University of Florida (graduated in December 2010). Dr. Joseph N. Wilson was my thesis adviser. I completed my under-graduation in Computer Science and Engineering from the College of Engineering, University of Kerala, Trivandrum, India in 2004.

Research Projects
Hyperparameter Selection in Probabilistic Topic Models
We are looking at different empirical Bayes methods for selecting hyperparameters in probabilistic topic models such as Latent Dirichlet allocation (Blei et al. 2003).
Electronic Legal Discovery
This project deals with modeling document collections from the Electronic Legal Discovery domain using different topic models such as latent Dirichlet allocation. We are also interested in improving the state-of-the-art of machine learning methods such as supervised classification, unsupervised clustering, and ranking employed in Electronic Discovery to reduce manual labor and increase investigative speed.

Past Research Projects
Survey Data Mining
We looked at a problem of automatic topic extraction, categorization, and relevance ranking model for surveys and their questions from different languages such as English, Spanish, Portuguese, German, and French. Automatically generated question and survey categories can be used to build question banks and category-specific survey templates. For this work, I collaborated with Dr. Joseph Wilson, Dr. Daisy Zhe Wang, Dr. Liana M. Epstein (SM), and Dr. Philip Garland (SM). This was a joint research project between the department of CISE and SurveyMonkey (SM) , a large scale online survey management system.
Topic Modeling
We looked at improving the traditional collapsed Gibbs sampling algorithm of the well known topic model latent Dirichlet allocation and came up with a 2-stage Gibbs sampler based on product partition models. A new topic inference algorithm for the newly encountered data points without retraining the learned model based on a Metropolis Hastings algorithm was developed. For this work, I collaborated with Dr. Paul Gader , Dr. Joseph Wilson, Dr. George Casella (late), Taylor Glenn, Dr. Claudio Fuentes, and Dr. Vikneshwaran Gopal.
Morpheus
I worked with Dr. Joseph N. Wilson and the Morpheus research team, to build a semantic question answering system by exploiting information from the DBpedia and Wikipedia along with sample query answering strategies provided by users.
